Aranmula Heritage Village

A Global Heritage Site on the Banks of River Pamba

The Soul of Pathanamthitta

Aranmula is a traditional village in Pathanamthitta district, world-renowned for its vast cultural contributions. It is one of the few places in India where the ancient traditions of craft and ritual are still practiced in their original form. From the mystical metal mirrors to the rhythmic snake boat races, Aranmula is a living museum.

The Aranmula Kannadi is a unique metal-alloy mirror. Unlike glass mirrors, the reflection comes from the front surface, eliminating distortion. Its composition remains a closely guarded family secret passed down through generations.

The famous Snake Boat Race is held during the Onam season. These "Palliyodams" (divine vessels) are rowed to the rhythmic songs (Vanchipattu) by hundreds of oarsmen in a display of communal harmony.

Temple & Valla Sadya

The ancient **Parthasarathy Temple** is the heart of the village. It is famous for the Valla Sadya, a grand ritualistic feast offered to the oarsmen of the snake boats, featuring over 60 traditional dishes.
