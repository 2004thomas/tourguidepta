Explore Pathanamthitta

The Land of Festivals & Hidden Gems

Gavi

Eco-tourism center nestled in Periyar Tiger Reserve. Famous for trekking and wildlife.

Aranmula

Cultural village home to the Aranmula Mirror and the famous snake boat race.

Orakkanpara

A serene, lesser-known waterfall hidden in the hills of Mallappally.

Konni & Adavi

Elephant training center and coracle rafting (bowl boat) in the Kallar River.

Manneera Waterfall

A beautiful hidden cascade near Seethathodu, perfect for a peaceful dip.

Sabarimala Temple

The world-famous hill shrine of Lord Ayyappa, located deep within the Periyar Tiger Reserve.

Aranmula Parthasarathy Temple

An ancient temple dedicated to Lord Krishna, famous for its Valla Sadya and architectural beauty.

Parumala Church

The tomb of Saint Gregorios of Parumala; it is a major center of pilgrimage for Christians worldwide.

Nilakkal Mahadeva Temple

An important 'Idathavalam' (halt points) for Sabarimala pilgrims and one of the oldest Shiva temples.

Pandalam Valiyakoikkal Temple

The palace where Lord Ayyappa is believed to have grown up. The holy ornaments (Thiruvabharanam) are kept here.

Charalkunnu Hill Station

Charalkunnu Hill Station is one of Pathanamthitta’s best place for refreshing, breezy climate and serves as a peaceful retreat from the bustling pilgrim towns below.

Anjumala Para

Also known as "Pathanamthitta’s Meesapulimala," it is a hidden gem that offers misty landscapes and a serene escape from urban life.

Chuttippara

Chuttippara is a majestic rocky outcrop rising about 200–300 feet right in the heart of Pathanamthitta town.

Chengara Viewpoint

Chengara Viewpoint (also known as Chengara Mist & Sunrise Viewpoint) is often called the "Munnar of Pathanamthitta."

Perunthenaruvi Waterfalls

Perunthenaruvi Waterfalls (literally "The Great Honey Stream") is one of the most spectacular natural attractions in Pathanamthitta.
