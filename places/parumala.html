Parumala Church

Sacred Pilgrimage Centre of Saint Geevarghese Mar Gregorios

About Parumala Church

Parumala Church, officially known as St. Peter’s and St. Paul’s Orthodox Church, is one of the most important pilgrimage centers of the Malankara Orthodox Syrian Church. Located on the banks of River Pamba near Thiruvalla, it is the final resting place of Saint Geevarghese Mar Gregorios (Parumala Thirumeni).

The church attracts thousands of devotees throughout the year, especially during the annual feast held in November. The peaceful riverside setting adds to the spiritual atmosphere of the place.

Spiritual Importance

  • Tomb of Saint Geevarghese Mar Gregorios
  • Major pilgrimage site for Orthodox Christians
  • Annual feast in November
  • Daily prayers and special services

Architecture & Features

  • Modern circular church structure
  • Beautiful altar and stained-glass interiors
  • Riverside landscape
  • Well-maintained prayer hall

How to Reach

Nearest Town: Thiruvalla (approx. 10 km)

Railway Station: Thiruvalla Railway Station

Airport: Cochin International Airport (approx. 110 km)

Well connected by road with regular bus services.

Essential Information

  • Open daily for visitors and prayer
  • Modest dress recommended
  • Peak crowd during November feast
  • Photography may be restricted inside

Annual Feast

The annual feast of Saint Geevarghese Mar Gregorios is celebrated in the first week of November. Devotees from across Kerala and outside the state participate in the special prayers and processions.

Nearby Facilities

  • Hotels and lodges in Thiruvalla town
  • Restaurants and bakeries nearby
  • Petrol pumps within 5–10 km radius
  • Hospitals in Thiruvalla

Visitor Guidelines

  • Maintain silence inside prayer hall
  • Respect religious customs
  • Follow instructions during feast days
  • Avoid crowding near sacred tomb area
