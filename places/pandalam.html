Pandalam Valiyakoikkal Temple

Sacred Birthplace of Lord Ayyappa

About Pandalam Temple

Pandalam Valiyakoikkal Temple is a historically and spiritually significant temple located in Pandalam town of Pathanamthitta district. It is closely associated with Lord Ayyappa and the royal family of Pandalam.

According to legend, Lord Ayyappa was raised as the prince of the Pandalam royal family. The temple complex and nearby Pandalam Palace are important pilgrimage spots for devotees before proceeding to Sabarimala.

Spiritual Significance

  • Associated with Lord Ayyappa’s childhood
  • Part of Sabarimala pilgrimage tradition
  • Royal family temple of Pandalam
  • Important during Mandala season

Key Highlights

  • Traditional Kerala temple architecture
  • Nearby Pandalam Palace
  • Sacred ornaments (Thiruvabharanam) procession
  • Peaceful riverside setting

How to Reach

Nearest Town: Pandalam

Railway Station: Chengannur (approx. 20 km)

Airport: Trivandrum International Airport (approx. 110 km)

Well connected by road from Pathanamthitta and Chengannur.

Essential Information

  • Open daily for devotees
  • Modest traditional attire recommended
  • Heavy crowd during Mandala season
  • Special rituals during festival periods

Connection to Sabarimala

The sacred ornaments (Thiruvabharanam) used in the Makaravilakku festival at Sabarimala are taken from Pandalam Palace in a ceremonial procession. This tradition makes Pandalam Temple an integral part of the Sabarimala pilgrimage.

Nearby Facilities

  • Hotels and lodges in Pandalam town
  • Restaurants and tea shops nearby
  • Petrol pumps within town limits
  • Hospitals in nearby towns

Visitor Guidelines

  • Maintain temple decorum and silence
  • Follow instructions from temple authorities
  • Avoid littering the premises
  • Be cautious during festival crowd rush
