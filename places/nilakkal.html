Nilakkal Mahadeva Temple

Ancient Shiva Temple Near Sabarimala

About Nilakkal Mahadeva Temple

Nilakkal Mahadeva Temple is an ancient Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, located near the base camp of Sabarimala in Pathanamthitta district. Surrounded by dense forests, the temple holds spiritual significance for devotees visiting Sabarimala.

It is believed to be centuries old and is associated with the ancient trade routes that once passed through this region. The peaceful forest setting adds to its sacred atmosphere.

Spiritual Significance

  • Dedicated to Lord Shiva
  • Visited by Sabarimala pilgrims
  • Important during Mandala-Makaravilakku season
  • Peaceful forest surroundings

Key Features

  • Traditional Kerala temple architecture
  • Simple yet serene shrine
  • Close proximity to Nilakkal Christian Church
  • Forest landscape and calm environment

How to Reach

Nearest Location: Nilakkal Base Camp

Railway Station: Chengannur (approx. 85 km)

Airport: Trivandrum International Airport (approx. 150 km)

Accessible by road via Sabarimala route.

Essential Information

  • Open during Sabarimala pilgrimage season
  • Temple timings may vary seasonally
  • Modest traditional dress recommended
  • Basic facilities available at Nilakkal base camp

Connection to Sabarimala

Many devotees visiting Sabarimala also stop at Nilakkal Mahadeva Temple to offer prayers before continuing their pilgrimage. It forms part of the spiritual journey for many pilgrims.

Nearby Facilities

  • Parking facilities at Nilakkal base camp
  • Temporary shops during pilgrimage season
  • Medical camps during peak season
  • Rest areas for pilgrims

Visitor Guidelines

  • Respect temple customs and rituals
  • Maintain silence inside temple premises
  • Follow instructions from authorities during pilgrimage season
  • Keep surroundings clean
