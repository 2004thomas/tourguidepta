Aranmula

Heritage Village on the Banks of River Pamba

About Aranmula

Aranmula is a historic and culturally rich village in Pathanamthitta district, situated along the banks of River Pamba. It is widely known for the Aranmula Parthasarathy Temple and the famous Aranmula Vallamkali (Snake Boat Race). The village beautifully blends spirituality, traditional art forms, and scenic river views, making it a must-visit heritage destination.

Key Attractions

  • Aranmula Parthasarathy Temple
  • Aranmula Uthrattathi Vallamkali (Snake Boat Race)
  • River Pamba views
  • Traditional Aranmula Kannadi (Metal Mirror)
  • Ancient Kerala architecture

Essential Information

  • Temple follows traditional dress code
  • Best time to visit: August–September (Boat race season)
  • Temple timings vary during festivals
  • Parking available near temple premises

How to Reach

Nearest Town: Pathanamthitta (approx. 10 km)

Railway Station: Chengannur (approx. 12 km)

Airport: Trivandrum International Airport (approx. 120 km)

Regular bus and taxi services are available from nearby towns.

Festivals & Cultural Importance

The Aranmula Vallamkali is one of Kerala’s oldest traditional boat races, held during Onam season. It is more of a devotional event than a competition. The temple is also one of the 108 Divya Desams dedicated to Lord Krishna.

Nearby Facilities

  • Hotels and lodges available in Pathanamthitta & Chengannur
  • Restaurants near temple area
  • Petrol pumps within 5–10 km radius
  • Hospitals in Pathanamthitta town

Safety Tips

  • Follow temple rules and dress code
  • Avoid overcrowded areas during festival peak hours
  • Respect local traditions and rituals
  • Be cautious near riverbanks during monsoon season
