Gavi

Eco-Tourism Paradise in the Western Ghats

About Gavi

Gavi is a serene eco-tourism destination located inside the Periyar Tiger Reserve in Pathanamthitta district. Surrounded by dense forests, grasslands, cardamom plantations, and hills, Gavi offers a peaceful escape into nature. It is one of the few places in Kerala where visitors can experience wildlife, trekking, and lake-side views in an undisturbed forest setting.

Key Attractions

  • Gavi Lake and boating experience
  • Forest safari inside Periyar Tiger Reserve
  • Bird watching (over 260 species recorded)
  • Panoramic viewpoints of Western Ghats
  • Cardamom and tea plantations

Essential Information

  • Entry permitted only with forest department approval
  • Limited daily visitor passes
  • Best time to visit: September to March
  • Mobile network coverage is weak

How to Reach

Nearest Town: Vandiperiyar (approx. 28 km)

Railway Station: Kottayam (approx. 140 km)

Airport: Kochi International Airport (approx. 170 km)

Visitors usually enter through Angamoozhy or Vallakkadavu forest check posts.

Trekking Routes

Guided trekking is available inside the forest reserve. Popular routes include short forest trails and hilltop viewpoints. Trekking is strictly monitored by forest officials to protect wildlife.

Booking & Entry Details

Entry to Gavi requires prior booking through the Kerala Forest Development Corporation (KFDC). Only limited vehicles are allowed daily to maintain ecological balance.

Safety Tips

  • Follow forest department guidelines strictly
  • Avoid littering and plastic usage
  • Do not attempt wildlife interaction
  • Carry water and basic medicines
  • Travel during daylight hours
