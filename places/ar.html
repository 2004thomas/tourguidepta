Aranmula Parthasarathy Temple

The Sacred Riverside Shrine of the Divine Charioteer

Heritage & History

The **Aranmula Parthasarathy Temple** is one of the most ancient temples in Kerala, situated on the banks of the holy Pamba River. Dedicated to Lord Krishna in his role as *Parthasarathy* (Arjuna's charioteer), the temple is built in the classic Kerala architectural style with magnificent wood carvings and murals.

It is the stopping point for the sacred **Thiruvabharanam** (sacred jewels of Lord Ayyappa) procession and is closely linked to the Sabarimala pilgrimage tradition.

Did you know?

The temple is built on high ground to protect it from the floods of the Pamba River. The eastern entrance is reached via a flight of 18 steps, while the northern entrance leads directly to the Pamba river bank where the snake boats are kept.
