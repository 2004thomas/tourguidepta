Sabarimala

Sacred Pilgrimage Shrine of Lord Ayyappa

About

Sabarimala, located in the Periyar Tiger Reserve of Pathanamthitta district, is one of the most significant pilgrimage centers in India. Dedicated to Lord Ayyappa, the temple attracts millions of devotees every year, especially during the Mandala-Makaravilakku season (November to January). The temple is situated amidst dense forests and hills, offering a unique spiritual and natural experience.

Key Highlights

  • Annual Mandala & Makaravilakku festival
  • Sacred 18 holy steps (Pathinettam Padi)
  • Forest trekking experience
  • Millions of devotees annually

How to Reach

Nearest Town: Pamba (base camp)

Railway: Chengannur (approx. 90 km)

Airport: Trivandrum (approx. 170 km)

From Pamba, pilgrims trek about 5 km uphill to reach the temple.

Trekking Route

The traditional trekking route starts from Pamba. The 5 km trek passes through forest terrain and concludes at the sacred 18 steps leading to the shrine. Pilgrims usually observe 41 days of vratham before visit.

Nearby Facilities

  • Medical centers available at Pamba
  • KSRTC transport services during season
  • Accommodation facilities at Pamba & Nilakkal
  • Parking & emergency services available
